# Target Audience

## Building a Personal Productivity System with AI

---

### Primary Audience

This course is designed for individuals who are **managing complex, multi-project lives** without a reliable system to keep them on track. They are motivated to improve but have been frustrated by productivity frameworks that don't stick.

**The course serves four core segments:**

---

### Segment 1: Early-Career Professionals (Ages 22–30)

**Who they are:**  
Recently graduated or in their first 2–3 years of employment. Juggling full-time work, side projects, skill development, and social obligations. They have energy but feel scattered.

**Pain points:**
- Tasks accumulate in their inbox or on sticky notes
- They feel busy but can't point to meaningful progress by end of week
- They start new tools every few months and abandon them shortly after
- They don't know how to prioritize when everything feels urgent

**What they want:**
- A system they can maintain in under 20 minutes a day
- Proof of progress they can point to (streaks, task logs, shipped work)
- An AI assistant they can actually use — not just play with

**Example persona:** *Priya, 25, junior marketing analyst. Has three active side projects, a backlog of unread newsletters, and a Notion page she hasn't opened in two months.*

---

### Segment 2: Freelancers (Ages 24–38)

**Who they are:**  
Independent workers managing multiple clients, project timelines, and their own business. They have no team telling them what to do — discipline and system design are their only infrastructure.

**Pain points:**
- Switching between client projects without a clear handoff protocol
- No visibility into where their hours are actually going
- Underestimating time required for recurring admin tasks
- No structured process for reviewing what went well vs. what didn't

**What they want:**
- A system that shows what they've delivered (anti-to-do, work session log)
- A weekly review structure that keeps them from drifting
- A way to plan the week without spending too long on meta-work

**Example persona:** *León, 31, UX designer. Working with 4 clients. Uses Trello for one client, WhatsApp for another, and his head for the rest. Keeps missing small deliverables.*

---

### Segment 3: Students (Ages 18–25)

**Who they are:**  
Undergraduate and postgraduate students who are managing coursework, part-time work, personal projects, and early-career networking. Time blindness and procrastination are their most common challenges.

**Pain points:**
- Can't tell if they're behind until they're very behind
- Struggle to translate big deadlines into daily actions
- Use task lists but not milestone thinking
- Feel guilty about unfinished tasks (anti-to-do directly addresses this)

**What they want:**
- A visual system that shows their progress clearly
- A way to plan backwards from exam or submission dates
- Context-appropriate AI assistance — not just "summarise this"

**Example persona:** *Aisha, 21, second-year economics undergraduate. Has exams in 6 weeks. Her current "system" is a WhatsApp message to herself.*

---

### Segment 4: Team Leads (Ages 28–42)

**Who they are:**  
Mid-level managers or senior individual contributors who manage their own workload AND need visibility into their team's work. They are experienced but increasingly overwhelmed as scope expands.

**Pain points:**
- Their personal system hasn't scaled with their responsibility
- They default to email and Slack as their task managers (neither works)
- No structured routine for personal performance retrospectives
- Lack of data to understand where they're actually spending their capacity

**What they want:**
- A system that respects their existing knowledge while adding structure
- A clear attention budget so they can protect deep work time
- A weekly review and retrospective that informs team priorities, not just personal ones

**Example persona:** *Marcus, 36, engineering team lead at a startup. Manages a team of 6. His personal productivity has not kept pace with his expanded role.*

---

### Audience Prerequisites

| Requirement | Level |
|-------------|-------|
| Technical skill | None required |
| Coding ability | Not needed |
| Prior Notion experience | Not needed — setup is guided from scratch |
| Prior AI/ChatGPT experience | Not needed — prompts are fully structured |
| Prior productivity system | Not needed — but learners with systems can refine theirs |

---

### Who This Course Is NOT For

- Teams looking for collaborative project management tools (this is personal PM, not team PM)
- Advanced users looking for Notion API integrations or automation scripting
- People looking for a time-management theory survey (this course builds, not lectures)
- Learners who are unwilling to share real goals and real projects during the course

---

### Recommended Group Size

| Setting | Recommended Size |
|---------|-----------------|

| Asynchronous self-paced | Unlimited |
| 1:1 coaching adaptation | Works well with minor adjustments |
