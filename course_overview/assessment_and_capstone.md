# Assessment and Capstone

## Building a Personal Productivity System with AI

---

### Assessment Philosophy

This course does not use traditional testing. There are no quizzes, no multiple-choice assessments, and no knowledge recitation exercises.

Assessment in this course is **entirely deliverable-based**. Learners demonstrate mastery by producing and presenting a real, working productivity system — built with their own goals, projects, and data.

The guiding principle: *If you built it and can explain why you built it that way, you have learned it.*

### Daily Deliverables (Formative)

These are not graded but are required for participation. They also serve as the building blocks of the capstone.

#### Day 1 Deliverables
- [ ] Goals Database with at least 3 goals, each with success criteria
- [ ] Projects Database with at least 2 active projects, each linked to a goal
- [ ] Task Database with at least 10 tasks, distributed across projects

#### Day 2 Deliverables
- [ ] Milestone map for at least 1 project (3+ milestones, reverse-planned)
- [ ] Prioritized task list using the AI prioritization prompt
- [ ] Anti-To-Do List with at least 5 completed items
- [ ] Attention Budget with current vs. ideal allocations defined

#### Day 3 Deliverables
- [ ] Kanban Board with Life OS workflow stages (6 stages), populated with tasks from at least 2 life areas
- [ ] Life OS Master Dashboard with at least 5 linked views (Today, Overdue, Due Soon, Active Projects, Completed This Week)
- [ ] Reminder Date property added to tasks; Due Soon and Overdue views working on dashboard
- [ ] Daily Check-In completed in Notion for Day 3
- [ ] Weekly Status view showing Completed This Week

### Capstone: Personal Productivity System Presentation

#### Overview

The capstone is a **10-minute presentation** of the learner's complete productivity system, followed by 5 minutes of Q&A or feedback. It takes place during Day 4 afternoon.

Learners share their screen and walk the evaluator (or group) through their Notion workspace, explaining the decisions they made at each stage.

#### What Learners Present

1. **Goals and the Why Behind Them** (2 min)  
   - Show the Goals Database
   - Explain one goal and its success criteria
   - Describe how they arrived at that definition using the AI prompt

2. **Projects and Milestone Map** (2 min)  
   - Show at least one project in detail
   - Walk through the milestone sequence
   - Explain the reverse-planning logic

3. **Task System and Prioritization** (2 min)  
   - Show the Task Database
   - Explain the priority tiering logic
   - Show the Anti-To-Do List and what it reveals about their actual output

4. **Life OS Dashboard Walkthrough** (2 min)  
   - Walk through the Life OS Master Dashboard
   - Show the Kanban board, Today view, Overdue view, and Due Soon view
   - Show the daily check-in toggle and most recent entry
   - Show the Completed This Week / weekly status view

5. **Daily AI Routine** (1 min)  
   - Describe their daily planning and end-of-day reflection ritual
   - Show an example output from their AI planning prompt
   - Describe how they plan to maintain the system after the course

6. **System Retrospective** (1 min)  
   - What they changed or would change from their initial design
   - One thing they learned about themselves through building this system

### Submission Requirements

Before the capstone presentation, learners submit:

1. **A screenshot or export** of their Notion workspace (or share a view-only Notion link)
2. **Capstone Written Walkthrough** — answers to 6 guided questions (template provided in [`capstone_instructions.md`](../student_materials/capstone_instructions.md))
3. **One completed Weekly Review** using the reflection template

These are submitted via the LMS or shared in the designated course folder before the Day 4 afternoon session.

---

### Instructor Notes on Assessment

- The capstone is not a performance — it is a guided walkthrough. Learners who are nervous should be told: *"Pretend you're showing a friend what you built."*
- Some learners will have beautifully structured systems; others will have messy but honest ones. Reward honesty and reasoning over polish.
- The retrospective section is the highest-signal indicator of real learning. A learner who says "I realised I was confusing tasks with projects and had to restructure everything" has learned more than one who has a perfect Notion setup but can't explain why it's designed that way.
