# Day 3 Minimum Setup Path

## Building a Personal Productivity System with AI

**Who this is for:**
Use this guide if you are joining on Day 3 without a complete Day 1–2 setup, OR if you only have a blank Notion workspace and want to follow along from scratch.

This guide gives you a working Life OS data foundation in approximately 20–30 minutes — just enough to participate fully in all Day 3 activities.

> **If you completed Days 1 and 2:** Skip this document entirely. Go directly to [`day_3_tasks.md`](../student_materials/student_task_sheets/day_3_tasks.md) — Track A.

---

## What You'll Have After This Setup

A single **Tasks database** in Notion with:
- The 6 Life OS status values (Inbox, Planned, This Week, In Progress, Waiting/Blocked, Done)
- Priority Property (High / Medium / Low)
- Due Date property
- Reminder Date property
- Area / Life Domain property
- 8–10 realistic sample tasks across at least 2 life areas

This is sufficient to build a fully functional Kanban, Life OS Dashboard, Due Soon view, Overdue view, Daily Check-In, and Weekly Status — everything on the Day 3 agenda.

## Step 1 — Create Your Notion Workspace

If you don't have a Notion account yet:
1. Go to [notion.so](https://notion.so)
2. Sign up with a Google account or email — it is free
3. Skip the onboarding tour (click "Start from scratch" or "Skip")
4. You will see a blank sidebar — this is your starting point

---

## Step 2 — Create Your Tasks Database

1. In the Notion left sidebar, click **"+ New page"**
2. Name it: `Tasks`
3. From the options that appear, select **"Table"** (this creates a database)
4. Notion will create a table with a default "Name" column and some placeholder rows — delete the placeholder rows now

---

## Step 3 — Add the Required Properties

Your Tasks database needs 6 properties. Add them in order:

### Property 1 — Task Name *(already exists)*
- This is the first "Name" column Notion creates automatically
- Rename it to "Task Name" if it isn't already

### Property 2 — Area / Life Domain
1. Click **"+"** to add a new property column
2. Type the name: `Area / Life Domain`
3. Choose property type: **Select**
4. Add these options (click each to add):
   - `Work`
   - `Studies`
   - `Personal`
   - `Content`
   - `Health`
   - `Admin`
   - `Finance`
5. Assign a different color to each option

### Property 3 — Status
1. Click **"+"** → type name: `Status`
2. Choose property type: **Select**
3. Add these 6 options (exact names matter — they power your Kanban filters):
   - `Inbox` → colour: Grey
   - `Planned` → colour: Blue
   - `This Week` → colour: Yellow
   - `In Progress` → colour: Purple
   - `Waiting / Blocked` → colour: Orange
   - `Done` → colour: Green

### Property 4 — Priority
1. Click **"+"** → type name: `Priority`
2. Choose property type: **Select**
3. Add these 3 options:
   - `High` → colour: Red
   - `Medium` → colour: Yellow
   - `Low` → colour: Grey

### Property 5 — Due Date
1. Click **"+"** → type name: `Due Date`
2. Choose property type: **Date**
3. Leave the format as default (Month/Day/Year or your local format)

### Property 6 — Reminder Date
1. Click **"+"** → type name: `Reminder Date`
2. Choose property type: **Date**
3. This is the date you want to be *reminded* — always before the Due Date

## Step 4 — Add Your Sample Tasks

You need at least 8 tasks across 2+ life areas. The more honest they are, the more useful Day 3 will be.

Add tasks by clicking **"+ New"** at the bottom of the database. Fill in all 5 properties for every task.

**Template: Copy and adapt these 10 sample tasks**

| Task Name | Area | Status | Priority | Due Date | Reminder Date |
|-----------|------|--------|----------|----------|---------------|
| Finish assignment draft | Studies | In Progress | High | [3 days from today] | [Tomorrow] |
| Email professor about extension | Studies | Planned | Medium | [Tomorrow] | [Today] |
| Reply to 3 unopened emails | Work / Admin | Inbox | Low | [This week] | [—] |
| Prepare for upcoming presentation | Work | This Week | High | [5 days from today] | [3 days from today] |
| Schedule dentist appointment | Health | Inbox | Medium | [2 weeks from today] | [1 week from today] |
| Gym session | Health | Planned | High | [Tomorrow] | [Today] |
| Review monthly budget | Finance | Planned | Medium | [End of this week] | [In 2 days] |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I add more tasks than 10?**
Yes — more is better. The dashboard is most useful when the data is real. Add anything you're actually tracking.

**Q: What if I don't have an "Area" for some tasks?**
Leave it blank for now. You can fill it in during the Day 3 Audit task. The dashboard still works — the Area filters just won't group as specifically.

**Q: Should I try to rebuild my Goals and Projects databases too?**
Not today. The minimum setup is intentionally one database so you can participate fully in Day 3. If you want to add Goals and Projects after the session, they're covered in the Day 1 and Day 2 materials.

**Q: Will my system be less complete than Track A learners?**
You'll build the same dashboard, Kanban, views, and daily check-in as Track A learners. The only difference is that your Tasks database isn't linked to Goals and Projects databases. You can add those after Day 3 using the Day 1–2 materials.
