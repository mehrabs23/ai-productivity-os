# Example: YouTube Creator Productivity System

## Building a Personal Productivity System with AI — Example Systems

---

**Persona:** Priya, 27 — part-time YouTube creator (personal finance niche), full-time bank associate, 22K subscribers, posting 2x per month

**Goal:** Grow to 100K subscribers and monetise channel to replace side-hustle income within 12 months.

---

### Priya's Situation at the Start of the Course

- Uploads 2 videos per month but feels like she's always running late on scripts
- Video ideas scattered across WhatsApp notes, a Google Doc, and her memory
- Zero system for audience research, thumbnail testing, or community management
- Spends 90% of her creative time on production, 0% on strategy or distribution
- Her biggest problem: she can't tell whether any given video performs well until it's been live 3 weeks

---

#### Attention Budget: Priya's Reality Check

| Domain | Ideal | Actual (Week 1) | Gap |
|--------|-------|----------------|-----|
| Content Production | 40% | 65% | -25% |
| Strategy / Ideas | 20% | 5% | +15% |
| Distribution / Community | 20% | 0% | +20% |

**AI Observation from the Attention Budget Prompt:**  
*"Distribution is receiving zero attention despite being named in your second-highest priority project. Strategy ideation is also almost absent. You're in full production mode — which produces videos but not an audience."*

**Priya's one change from this analysis:**  
> Protect Friday 7–9pm for strategy and distribution work. No production on Fridays.

---

#### Her Daily Planning Routine

Every Monday morning before opening any social media:
1. Open Notion dashboard
2. Identify what must be done in content production this week
3. Run the Morning Planning Prompt with: available hours (evenings only — 2hrs/day weekdays, 8hrs Saturday)
4. Block Saturday as a deep production day (minimum 6 hours)
5. Save plan to Monday's Daily Note

### Prompt Sequence Priya Used

1. **Goal Definition Prompt** → Found out her "100K" goal didn't have a meaningful intermediate milestone
2. **Milestone Generation Prompt** → Broke content pipeline into filming/editing/publishing checkpoints
3. **Reverse Planning Prompt** → Confirmed her January publish date requires an unrealistic script-to-edit turnaround (solved by moving publish to 28th)
4. **Attention Budget Prompt** → Revealed distribution black hole
5. **Daily Planning Prompt** → Saved 20 minutes per Monday; replaced chaotic "I'll figure it out" approach
6. **System Retrospective Prompt** → Stripped dashboard from 6 sections to 2; added SEO learning task to Friday block

## Day 3: Priya's Life OS Operationalized

### Priya's Life OS Dashboard

Priya named the dashboard: **🎬 Priya's Creator OS**

"Creator OS" — Priya's system is built around dual lives: full-time bank associate by day, content creator by evening and weekend.

**Dashboard layout (2-column, full-width):**

| Column 1 | Column 2 |
|----------|----------|
| 📝 Daily Check-In (toggle, top) | 📝 Daily Check-In continued |
| 🎯 Today (video production tasks + bank work) | ⚠️ Overdue |
| 📅 This Week | 🔔 Due Soon |

**Below fold:**
- 🗂️ Kanban — Active (Creator + Bank + Personal)
- ✅ Completed Recently
- 📊 Week in Review

**Life areas on Priya's Kanban:**

| Area | Example tasks |
|------|--------------|
| Content Production | "Script Video 3", "Record talking head — Video 2", "Edit Saturday session" |
| Strategy | "Find 3 Reddit communities for distribution", "Analyse top-performing February video" |
| Bank Work | "Prepare Q1 client review pack", "Submit timesheet" |
| Admin | "Chase affiliate partnership invoice", "Reply to brand deal enquiry" |
| Personal | "GP check-up", "Call parents Sunday" |

### Priya's Reminder Setup

Priya's most critical reminder: video publish dates.

She sets a Due Date on each video's "Publish" task and a Reminder Date 4 days earlier (to leave time for thumbnail, SEO, and scheduling).

> *"Before, I'd suddenly realize on a Thursday that a video was meant to go live Saturday and I hadn't done the thumbnail. Now I see it in 'Due Soon' on Monday. I actually have time to prepare."*

Priya's **Overdue view** caught a distribution task that had been quietly slipping for 2 weeks — Reddit posting she said she'd do bi-weekly but hadn't done once. Naming it as overdue forced her to address it.

**Her Due Soon view** shows the next video publish milestone and her Friday strategy block task. She checks both every Monday morning before work.
