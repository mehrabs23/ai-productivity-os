# Capstone Instructions

## Building a Personal Productivity System with AI

---

### Overview

The capstone is the final act of this course. You will give a **10-minute walkthrough** of your complete productivity system to an evaluator or small group, followed by 5 minutes of feedback or Q&A.

The capstone is not a performance. It is a guided tour of something you built. Your job is to show what you made and explain why you designed it that way.

---

### What the Capstone Is NOT

- Not a slide deck or presentation (you can have one if you want, but it's not required)
- Not a report or essay (it's a live walkthrough of your actual system)
- Not a test of how "complete" your system is (it's a test of how well you understand your own system)
- Not something you need to polish for hours (build it well, then present it honestly)

---

### What You Will Show

Walk through your Notion workspace in this order. Each section has a suggested time allocation:

---

#### Section 1: Your Goals (2 minutes)

**Open:** Goals Database

**Show and explain:**
- Open your Goals Database
- Select your most important goal
- Read the goal name and outcome statement aloud
- Walk through the success criteria: "I'll know I've achieved this goal when..."
- Explain briefly: "Here's how I arrived at this definition *(mention the AI prompting process)*"

**Evaluator is looking for:**
- Specificity: Is the success criteria observable and measurable?
- Ownership: Does the learner clearly understand and own this goal?
- Process: Can they describe how the AI prompt helped them clarify it?

---

#### Section 2: Projects and Milestones (2 minutes)

**Open:** Projects Database → then a single project detail page

**Show and explain:**
- Navigate to your most active project
- Show the link to its parent goal
- Show the milestone map: 3+ milestones with target dates
- Explain the reverse-planning logic: "I worked backwards from [end date] to figure out these milestones"
- Point to one milestone and explain: "This milestone exists because [reason]"

**Evaluator is looking for:**
- Clear project scope
- Milestones that are checkpoints, not tasks
- Realistic date sequencing

---

#### Section 3: Task System and Prioritization (2 minutes)

**Open:** Task Database (filtered view: This Week's Priorities OR Kanban Board)

**Show and explain:**
- Navigate to your "This Week's Priorities" view or your Kanban board
- Explain your priority tier system: "P1 means X, P2 means Y..."
- Show your Anti-To-Do List: "This captures what I've actually completed"
- Point to 2–3 tasks and explain why they're at their current priority level

**Evaluator is looking for:**
- Consistent priority logic
- Anti-To-Do with genuine entries
- Understanding of priority tiering

---

#### Section 4: Dashboard Walkthrough (2 minutes)

**Open:** Master Dashboard

**Show and explain:**
- Open your Master Dashboard (Mission Control page)
- Show each section and explain what question it answers
- Show your Kanban board with tasks distributed across stages
- Show your Streak Tracker with at least 2 habits tracked
- Do the load test live: "Can I find today's top priority in 5 seconds? Yes / Here's why not"

**Evaluator is looking for:**
- Dashboard that aggregates useful views
- Kanban with meaningful stages
- Streak tracker with process habits (not outcome targets)
- Evidence of UX thought (fewer sections = better, if each section is useful)

---

#### Section 5: Daily AI Planning Routine (1 minute)

**Open:** Daily Note from today or yesterday

**Show and explain:**
- Open the Notion Daily Note from today's or yesterday's planning session
- Show the AI-generated plan (or the plan you generated using the prompt)
- Explain: "This is what my planning routine produces — it takes about 10 minutes"
- Briefly describe how you plan to maintain this ritual after the course

**Evaluator is looking for:**
- Evidence that the learner has actually run the planning routine (not just described it)
- Understanding of the ritual's value

---

#### Section 6: System Retrospective (1 minute)

**Show and explain:**
- Describe one thing you would change about your system design if you started again
- Describe one thing that surprised you about your own working habits through building this system
- Describe the one concrete improvement you identified in your Day 4 retrospective

**Evaluator is looking for:**
- Honest, specific retrospective (not generic praise of the course)
- Evidence of meta-awareness: the learner understands their own system and its limitations

### Capstone Written Walkthrough

Answer these 6 questions in writing. Submit before your presentation. Length: 1–3 sentences per question.

**Q1.** What is your most important goal, and how did the AI prompting process change how you defined it?

**Q2.** What is your active project most at risk right now — and what is the next milestone that would move it forward?

**Q3.** What did your attention budget reveal about how you're actually spending your time?

**Q4.** What is the design decision you're most proud of in your dashboard — and why?

**Q5.** Describe your daily planning ritual in 2 sentences, as if you were explaining it to a colleague.

**Q6.** What is the one thing you would change about your system if you started the course over?

### A Note on Nerves

Some learners feel anxious about presenting. Here's the reframe:

> *You are not being evaluated on whether your system is perfect. You are being evaluated on whether you understand your own system and can explain the thinking behind it.*

An honest, partly-built system presented with clear reasoning will score higher than a polished system the presenter can't explain.

"I chose to structure my priorities this way because..." is the sentence that will serve you best.
