# Final Capstone Rubric

## Building a Personal Productivity System with AI

---

### Rubric Overview

**Total: 100 points | Pass threshold: 70 points**

The rubric is applied during the 10-minute capstone presentation and assessed against the submitted Capstone Written Walkthrough. Evaluators should award points based on observable evidence — not on how polished the system looks, but on how clearly the learner understands and can explain their design choices.

---

## Criterion 1: Goals Database Quality (15 points)

**What is being evaluated:**  
Whether each goal has a specific outcome statement, a clear deadline, and success criteria that define what "winning" looks like.

| Band | Points | Descriptor |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Excellent | 13–15 | 3+ goals present. Each has a specific, observable outcome statement. Success criteria are verifiable — you could tell unambiguously whether the goal was achieved. Learner can explain at least one goal with clarity and ownership. AI prompt process is visible (goal was refined from a raw statement). |
| Proficient | 9–12 | 2+ goals present. Most have success criteria, though one or more may be vague or too broad. Learner can describe their goals and understands the structure. Minor gaps in specificity. |
| Developing | 5–8 | Goals present but success criteria are generic ("I will be more productive") or aspirational without measurable markers. Learner may struggle to explain what "done" looks like. |
| Beginning | 0–4 | Goals database is empty or contains only task-level items. No success criteria. Learner cannot connect any goal to a measurable outcome. |

---

## Criterion 2: Projects and Milestone Structure (15 points)

**What is being evaluated:**  
Whether projects are clearly scoped, linked to goals, and have a milestone map that reflects reverse-planning thinking.

| Band | Points | Descriptor |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Excellent | 13–15 | 2+ active projects, each linked to a goal. At least one project has 3+ milestones with target dates derived from reverse planning. Learner can explain the milestone logic: "This milestone must happen before X because..." |
| Proficient | 9–12 | 2+ projects. Most have at least 2 milestones. Dates are assigned, though the reverse-planning logic may not be fully articulated. Projects are clearly scoped. |
| Developing | 5–8 | Projects present but milestones are task-level, undated, or in only one project. Learner has partial understanding of milestone thinking. |
| Beginning | 0–4 | Projects database lacks milestones, or milestones are not linked to project goals. Learner struggles to distinguish milestones from tasks. |

---

## Criterion 3: Task System and Prioritization (15 points)

**What is being evaluated:**  
Whether tasks are linked to projects, prioritized using a consistent logic, and whether an Anti-To-Do List is present and used.

| Band | Points | Descriptor |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Excellent | 13–15 | 10+ tasks present, linked to projects. Priority tiers are applied consistently (not everything marked High). Learner can explain the reasoning for at least 2 priority decisions. Anti-To-Do has 5+ genuine entries with clear categories. Learner can describe how the Anti-To-Do feeds their weekly review. |
| Proficient | 9–12 | 8+ tasks with priorities. Mostly consistent, with 1–2 edge cases. Anti-To-Do present with 3+ entries. Learner understands the purpose. |
| Developing | 5–8 | Tasks present but not all linked to projects. Priorities applied but without clear logic. Anti-To-Do has 1–2 entries or duplicates the task list. |
| Beginning | 0–4 | Task database sparsely populated. Priority labelling is inconsistent or all tasks marked as same priority. Anti-To-Do absent or unused. |

---

## Criterion 4: Dashboard Design and Usability (15 points)

**What is being evaluated:**  
Whether the Master Dashboard aggregates useful views, eliminates noise, and enables the learner to answer "what do I do today?" within 5 seconds.

| Band | Points | Descriptor |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Excellent | 13–15 | Dashboard has 3+ linked views, each serving a clear purpose. Load test passed (top priority visible in ≤5 sec). Kanban board has 4+ stages with tasks distributed meaningfully. Streak Tracker has 2+ process habits with today's entries. Learner can explain every section's purpose and has removed at least one section from audit feedback. |
| Proficient | 9–12 | Dashboard has 2–3 views. Load test mostly passes. Kanban present and populated. Streak Tracker set up. Minor UX issues (redundant sections, unclear labels). |
| Developing | 5–8 | Dashboard present but some linked views are non-functional or link to wrong databases. Kanban is set up but sparsely used. Streak Tracker is empty or has only one habit. |
| Beginning | 0–4 | Master Dashboard is absent or is a basic notes page. No linked views. Kanban or Streak Tracker absent. |

---

## Criterion 5: AI Prompt Integration (15 points)

**What is being evaluated:**  
Whether learner can demonstrate that they used structured prompts from the prompt library to produce better outputs than they would have created unaided — and whether they edited the output.

| Band | Points | Descriptor |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Excellent | 13–15 | Learner demonstrates use of 3+ distinct prompts (e.g., goal design, task breakdown, daily planning). For at least one prompt, they can show the original output AND explain what they changed and why. Learner articulates the "review and edit" step clearly. |
| Proficient | 9–12 | Learner used 2 prompts and can describe the output and at least one edit. May not be able to distinguish AI output from their own additions. |
| Developing | 5–8 | Learner used 1 prompt. Can describe what it produced but cannot clearly distinguish their edits from the AI output. May have accepted the AI output uncritically. |
| Beginning | 0–4 | Learner cannot demonstrate any specific AI prompt use. System appears to have been built without AI involvement, or learner cannot describe the prompting process. |

---

## Criterion 6: Daily Planning Routine (10 points)

**What is being evaluated:**  
Whether learner has a working daily planning routine they can describe and demonstrate.

| Band | Points | Descriptor |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Excellent | 9–10 | Learner shows a Daily Note from an actual planning session. Describes the ritual clearly (time, prompt, output). Has scheduled a recurring reminder. Can explain why the ritual is valuable vs. ad hoc planning. |
| Proficient | 7–8 | Learner ran the planning session during the course and can describe it. May not have committed to a post-course schedule yet. |
| Developing | 4–6 | Learner understands the concept but has not run a full session. Can describe the steps but lacks a personal example. |
| Beginning | 0–3 | Learner cannot demonstrate or describe a daily planning routine. No Daily Note present. |

---

## Criterion 7: Retrospective Quality (10 points)

**What is being evaluated:**  
Whether learner can honestly assess what worked and what didn't in their system, and propose a concrete improvement.

| Band | Points | Descriptor |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Excellent | 9–10 | Learner identifies a specific design decision that didn't work as expected and explains why. Proposes one concrete change (not vague, e.g., "use it more"). Demonstrates meta-awareness: they understand the system as a design to be iterated. |
| Proficient | 7–8 | Learner can identify what they'd change with some specificity. May be more generic than ideal but shows honest self-assessment. |
| Developing | 4–6 | Retrospective is primarily positive ("everything worked well"). Learner is reluctant to identify weaknesses or cannot articulate specific improvements. |
| Beginning | 0–3 | Learner cannot engage with the retrospective. No self-assessment beyond "it was good/bad". |

---

## Criterion 8: Presentation Clarity and Confidence (5 points)

**What is being evaluated:**  
Whether the learner can navigate and explain their system fluidly in 10 minutes.

| Band | Points | Descriptor |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Excellent | 5 | Presentation is structured and within time. Learner navigates Notion confidently. Explanations are clear and use course vocabulary correctly. |
| Proficient | 3–4 | Minor navigation fumbles or slight over/under on time. Clear explanations in most sections. |
| Developing | 1–2 | Significant navigation difficulty or time overrun. Some explanations are unclear or use vague language. |
| Beginning | 0 | Learner was unable to present in an organised way or did not attend the capstone. |
