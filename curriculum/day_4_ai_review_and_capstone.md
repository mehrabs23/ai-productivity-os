## Session 1: Daily AI Planning Routine (60 minutes)

### Teaching Portion (20 minutes)

**Concept: Planning as a Daily Operating Procedure**

Most people start their day reactively — they open email, see what's on fire, and respond. By the time they get to intentional work, the day is already fragmented.

A **daily AI planning routine** is a 10–15 minute morning ritual that answers four questions before the first task begins:

1. **Context:** What is my current state? (energy, mood, hours available)
2. **Priorities:** What are the 3 things that must move today?
3. **Blockers:** What might stop me from completing those 3 things?
4. **Plan:** What is my realistic sequence for the day?

With ChatGPT, this ritual becomes a structured conversation — the learner provides honest input, the AI returns a structured daily plan. The learner edits and commits.

**The Daily Planning Prompt Sequence (from prompt library):**

**Step 1 — Context Input:**  
Tell ChatGPT your available hours, current energy level, and top 3 priorities from your Notion dashboard.

**Step 2 — AI Response:**  
ChatGPT acknowledges constraints, identifies potential blockers, and proposes a time-blocked sequence for the day.

**Step 3 — Learner Review:**  
Read the plan critically. Is anything missing? Is this realistic? Add or remove items.

**Step 4 — Commit:**  
Copy the finalised plan into a daily note in Notion (or a sticky note, or a blank document). This is the day's operating plan.

**Why this works:**  
Writing down a plan — even an imperfect one — dramatically increases the probability of following it. AI accelerates the planning phase. Ownership remains with the learner.

### Demo (20 minutes)

**Instructor demonstrates a full morning planning session with Alex:**

1. Shows Alex's Notion dashboard (from Day 3)
2. Reads off Alex's top priorities and current state from the dashboard
3. Opens ChatGPT and runs the Daily Planning Prompt (Step 1)
4. Reviews the AI output — a time-blocked day plan
5. Edits the plan: moves one task to tomorrow (realistic given Alex's meeting), adds a task the AI missed
6. Copies the finalised plan into a Notion Daily Note
7. Discusses: "This took 12 minutes. It replaces 30 minutes of low-level anxiety."

### Student Task 1A: Run Your Daily Planning Session (40 minutes)

**Minimum Viable Task:**
1. Open your Notion dashboard
2. Identify your top 3 priorities for today
3. Note your available hours and current energy level (honest estimate)
4. Use the Daily Planning Prompt from the prompt library (fill in the template with your real context)
5. Review and edit the ChatGPT output — personalise it
6. Copy the finalised plan into a Notion Daily Note for today
7. Set a recurring calendar reminder for 8:30am every workday: "Morning Planning (15 min)"

**Extension Task:**
- Experiment with the Daily Planning Prompt variation that asks ChatGPT to also flag potential energy mismatches — e.g., scheduling complex cognitive work during your lowest-energy period
- Create a reusable "Planning Note" template in Notion to use each morning

**Deliverable:** A completed daily plan for today, stored in a Notion Daily Note

## Session 2: Overload Triage & End-of-Day Reflection (60 minutes)

### Teaching Portion (20 minutes)

**Concept 1: Overload Triage**

Most productivity failures happen not when people are lazy but when they're overwhelmed. When a task list has 20+ items and all seem urgent, forward momentum breaks down. Paralysis sets in.

The **Overload Triage Prompt** is used when the task list exceeds what is realistically doable today or this week. It forces explicit prioritization decisions by asking ChatGPT to evaluate each task against:
- Consequence of delay
- Time required
- Dependency (is anything blocked by this?)
- Reversibility (can this be undone or recovered if skipped?)

The output is a triage classification:
- **Do now** — must happen today, non-negotiable
- **Schedule** — important but can be scheduled for a specific future date
- **Delegate** — could be done by someone else (or noted as a candidate for delegation)
- **Drop** — low consequence, can be explicitly removed from the list

**The key mindset shift:**  
Deciding to NOT do something is a decision, not a failure. The triage prompt makes deferral and dropping explicit and intentional — rather than passive.

**Concept 2: End-of-Day Reflection**

The end-of-day reflection is a 5–10 minute ritual at the end of each workday that closes the mental loop. It answers:

1. What did I ship / complete today?
2. What didn't move that I expected to move?
3. What surprised me?
4. What do I want to remember for tomorrow?
5. On a scale of 1–10, how focused was I today — and why?

This is the daily check-in template in action. It feeds directly into:
- The Anti-To-Do List (what to add)
- The Work Session Log (log today's sessions)
- The Week's review (raw material)

**Why close the loop:**  
Without an end-of-day ritual, work bleeds into evenings. Incompletions occupy working memory. The ritual signals: "Work is done. I know where things stand. I can let go."

### Demo (15 minutes)

**Instructor demonstrates two prompts:**

**Overload Triage:**
1. Shows a fictional overwhelmed task list with 15 items (Alex's "everyone wants something" Monday)
2. Uses the Overload Triage Prompt
3. AI classifies each task; instructor reviews and disagrees with one classification
4. Result: 3 tasks for today, 5 scheduled for later this week, 2 delegated, 5 dropped (with brief reason)

**End-of-Day Reflection:**
1. Uses the Daily Check-In template as a structured 5-question prompt
2. Completes it as Alex, using fictional day data
3. Shows how the anti-to-do and session log entries are derived from the reflection

### Student Task 2A: Triage + Reflect (45 minutes)

**Minimum Viable Task (Triage):**
1. Export or copy your current task list (all tasks, not just today's)
2. Run the Overload Triage Prompt if your list has more than 10 active tasks
3. Apply the triage decisions: move "Scheduled" tasks to future due dates, mark "Drop" tasks as archived
4. Document your triage decisions using a simple note in your Notion workspace

**Minimum Viable Task (Reflection):**
1. Complete the Day 4 Daily Check-In using the template
2. Add at least 3 entries to your Anti-To-Do from today's work
3. Log today's work sessions in the Work Session Log (even if approximate)

**Extension Task:**
- Create a recurring "End of Day" note template in Notion with the 5 reflection questions pre-filled
- Set a calendar reminder for 5:30pm every workday: "End-of-Day Reflection (10 min)"

**Deliverable:** Completed Daily Check-In for Day 4 + updated Anti-To-Do and Work Session Log

## Session 3: Weekly Review & System Retrospective (60 minutes)

### Teaching Portion (25 minutes)

**Concept 1: The Weekly Review Ritual**

The weekly review is a 20–30 minute weekly ritual (typically Friday afternoon or Sunday evening) that closes the week and prepares for the next. It is distinct from the daily check-in because it operates at a higher level of abstraction — it looks at patterns, not just individual days.

**The 6-step weekly review sequence:**

1. **Clear the decks:** Close all open tabs, process all inboxes to zero
2. **Review the anti-to-do:** What actually shipped this week?
3. **Review open tasks:** What was planned but not done? Why?
4. **Review active projects:** Which projects moved? Which are stalled?
5. **Check the attention budget:** Actual vs. ideal — what was the largest gap?
6. **Plan next week:** Set the 3 most important outcomes for next week

**The Weekly Review Prompt (from prompt library):**  
The learner provides the previous week's anti-to-do entries, incomplete tasks, and attention allocation. ChatGPT generates a structured weekly summary, identifies patterns, and proposes adjustments for next week.

**Concept 2: The System Retrospective**

A retrospective is not about what you did — it's about how well your system is working. It asks:

- What parts of my system am I actually using?
- What parts did I set up but abandoned?
- What is the weakest link in my workflow?
- What is one change I will make to improve the system?

This is borrowed from Agile development practice. Every 2–4 weeks, a brief retrospective prevents system decay and keeps the productivity OS alive.

**The System Retrospective Prompt** asks ChatGPT to analyse the learner's system description and identify friction points, underused components, and improvement opportunities.

### Demo (20 minutes)

**Instructor demonstrates a full Weekly Review with Alex's fictional week:**

1. Opens Alex's Anti-To-Do database — reads off the week's entries
2. Reviews open tasks — 4 tasks that didn't get done; identifies one pattern (all admin tasks)
3. Checks attention allocation — admin consumed 35% vs. ideal 10%
4. Runs the Weekly Review Prompt — AI summarizes the week and recommends: "Move recurring admin to one consolidated admin block on Tuesdays"
5. Sets 3 outcomes for next week in a Notion "Next Week" note

**System Retrospective:**
1. Describes Alex's system: which components were used, which were ignored
2. Runs the System Retrospective Prompt
3. AI identifies: "Anti-To-Do entries decreased from Day 3 — is the habit being maintained?"
4. Instructor proposes one improvement: add Anti-To-Do to the daily reminder checklist

### Student Task 3A: Run Your Weekly Review & Retrospective (40 minutes)

**Minimum Viable Task (Weekly Review):**
1. Complete all 6 steps of the weekly review sequence
2. Use the Weekly Review Prompt with your real data from this week (anti-to-do, open tasks, attention allocation)
3. Set your 3 most important outcomes for next week
4. Create a "Weekly Review" note in Notion with the date and your answers

**Minimum Viable Task (Retrospective):**
1. Answer all 4 retrospective questions in writing
2. Use the System Retrospective Prompt to get AI-generated friction analysis
3. Identify ONE concrete change you will make to your system
4. Document the change in your Notion workspace (update a database property, add a note, or adjust a view)

**Extension Task:**
- Schedule a recurring 30-minute "Weekly Review" event on your calendar for every Friday at 4pm
- Add a retrospective check-in rule: "Every 4 weeks, I will run the System Retrospective Prompt and implement at least one change"

**Deliverable:** Completed Weekly Review note + written retrospective with one documented system improvement
