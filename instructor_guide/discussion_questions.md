# Discussion Questions

## Building a Personal Productivity System with AI

---

### Overview

These curated discussion questions are used during pair shares, group discussions, and closing reflections across all four days. Each question is designed to surface insight, expose assumptions, and press learners to connect the course content to their actual situation.

Use these questions as prompts, not scripts. The best discussions emerge from a question followed by silence — resist the urge to fill the silence.

---

## Day 1 Discussion Questions

### Session 1: What Is a Productivity OS?

**Q1.1.** "What is the biggest gap in how you currently manage your work? Not what you hope to fix — what is the actual, specific thing that goes wrong most often?"

**Q1.2.** "Think about the last time you felt genuinely productive. What conditions made that possible? What would need to happen to make those conditions your default?"

**Q1.3.** "What 'system' were you using before this course — even if you wouldn't have called it a system? What made it work? What made it fail?"

---

### Session 2: Defining Goals with AI

**Q2.1.** *(Pair share)* "Read your most important goal out loud to your partner. Then ask: does the success criteria actually tell you when you've won? Give each other honest feedback."

**Q2.2.** "What's the difference between a goal you wrote down because you genuinely want it, and a goal you wrote down because you think you should want it? Which ones are in your Goals Database right now?"

**Q2.3.** "What goal have you been carrying around in your head for more than 6 months without writing it down? Why hasn't it been committed to paper until now?"

---

### Session 4: Tasks

**Q4.1.** "Look at your task list. How many of your tasks will take more than one work session to complete? How could you split them?"

**Q4.2.** "What type of task do you consistently add to your list — and then consistently not do? What does that pattern reveal?"

---

## Day 2 Discussion Questions

### Session 1: Milestone Thinking

**Q5.1.** "What is the current state of your most important project? If someone asked you what percentage complete it is, what would you say — and how confident are you in that number?"

**Q5.2.** "What's the difference between a milestone and a due date? Why does that distinction matter?"

**Q5.3.** *(Pair check)* "Share your milestone map for one project with your partner. Ask them: 'Does this feel realistic? Are there any gaps?'"

---

### Session 2: Prioritization

**Q6.1.** "How many of your tasks did you label P1? If it's more than two, what does that tell you about how 'priority' was actually being assigned before this course?"

**Q6.2.** "What task have you been deferring because everything else feels more urgent? Why has it not escalated to P1, and what are the consequences if it keeps being deferred?"

**Q6.3.** "What's the difference between something being genuinely high priority and something just feeling urgent because of social pressure?"

---

### Session 3: Anti-To-Do

**Q7.1.** "What work do you do every week that never appears on any to-do list? What would change if that work was visible?"

**Q7.2.** "Did anything surprise you when you started listing what you'd actually done in the past three days? What kind of work do you undercount?"

**Q7.3.** "When you have a 'bad' productivity day, what does it look like? How would your anti-to-do tell a different story about that day?"

---

### Session 4: Attention Budget

**Q8.1.** "What is the biggest gap between your ideal attention allocation and your actual allocation? Did you already know this before today?"

**Q8.2.** "What activity is consuming attention that you never consciously chose to give it? How did it end up getting so much of your time?"

**Q8.3.** "If you could protect just two hours of focused time per day for your most important project, what would need to change in your current schedule?"

---

## Day 3 Discussion Questions

### Session 2: Life OS Kanban

**Q9.1.** "Look at your Kanban board. What does the distribution of cards across your stages tell you? Which stage has the most cards in it — and is that a problem?"

**Q9.2.** "What task in your system is blocked right now? What would need to happen to unblock it, and is that in your control?"

**Q9.3.** "Across which areas of your life are you currently most overloaded? Does your Kanban make that visible, or does it hide it?"

---

### Session 3: Life OS Dashboard

**Q10.1.** "Open your dashboard and run the 5-second test: within 5 seconds, can you tell what your most important task is today? If not — what is getting in the way?"

**Q10.2.** "Which section of your dashboard do you predict you'll never open after this course? Should you remove it now, before it makes the dashboard feel heavy?"

**Q10.3.** "Does your dashboard currently show you what's overdue? If you opened it right now, would you know immediately if something is late?"

---

### Session 4: Reminders & Due Soon View

**Q11.1.** "How do you currently remember things that are coming up in the next 2–3 days — before they become urgent? Is that system reliable?"

**Q11.2.** "What's the task or commitment that, historically, you've been most likely to forget or delay because you didn't notice its deadline approaching? How would a Due Soon view have helped?"

---

### Session 5: Daily Check-In

**Q12.1.** "How did it feel to complete the daily check-in? What was missing from how you usually end your day that this process made visible?"

**Q12.2.** "What is the one question in the daily check-in you found hardest to answer honestly? Why do you think that is?"

---

### Session 6: Weekly Status & Progress Sharing

**Q13.1.** *(Peer review)* "Share your 'Completed This Week' view with a partner for 60 seconds. Give them one observation: 'The pattern I notice in what you shipped this week is...' Then swap."

**Q13.2.** "If you had to send your weekly status view to one person — a mentor, manager, or accountability partner — what would you want them to notice? What would you be nervous about them seeing?"

---

## Day 4 Discussion Questions

### Session 1: Daily Planning

**Q13.1.** "What did the AI daily planning session reveal about your current task list that you hadn't noticed before? Was any task missing from your original priorities?"

**Q13.2.** "What would your mornings need to look like for you to consistently run a 10-minute planning session before starting reactive work?"

---

### Session 3: Weekly Review

**Q14.1.** "Are there things you accomplished this week that went unrecognised — by yourself or others — that only show up in your anti-to-do? What's the value of having that visible?"

**Q14.2.** "What did your system retrospective reveal about the gap between your intended system design and how you actually used it? Which part surprised you most?"

---

### Session 4: Capstone / Course Close

**Q15.1.** "What did you learn about yourself — not just about productivity — through building this system?"

**Q15.2.** "Which part of your system are you most proud of? Which part are you least confident will survive the first week after this course?"

**Q15.3.** "If a colleague asked you in three months 'do you still use that course system?', what would you need to implement this week to ensure the answer is yes?"
